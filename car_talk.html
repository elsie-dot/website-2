Tyre Types and what they do:

Tyre Type Colour When are they used
Soft Red For maximum grip and the fastest lap times, commonly for qualifying sessions and short, fast race stints
Medium Yellow Used in dry conditions, they are slower than soft tyres but last longer
Hard White Used in dry conditions, they are the slowest and take longer to warm up but last the longest
Intermediate Green Used in light rain, they have grooves to disperse water and prevent aquaplaning
Wet Blue Used in heavy rain, they have deeper grooves to disperse more water and provide better grip

What does the back wing accomplish?

The rear wing of a Formula 1 car is designed to generate downforce, which helps to increase the car's grip on the track.

The rear wing works by creating a pressure difference between the upper and lower surfaces of the wing. As air flows over the wing, it is forced to travel faster over the upper surface than the lower surface, creating a low-pressure area above the wing and a high-pressure area below it. This pressure difference generates downforce, which pushes the car down onto the track and allows it to corner at higher speeds without losing traction.

In addition to generating downforce, the rear wing also helps to reduce drag, which is the resistance that the car experiences as it moves through the air. The rear wing is designed to be as aerodynamic as possible, with a shape that minimizes drag while still providing enough downforce to keep the car stable at high speeds.

The drivers are able to activate a DRS Mode which open up the wing which boots the cars on straights

Frequently asked questions

How much does a Formula 1 car weigh?

The minimum weight for a Formula 1 car is 798 kg (1,759 lbs), including the driver and safety equipment.

Why are some cars faster than others? And what are the rules for making a car?

Each team interprets complex technical regulations differently, designing their own unique chassis, aerodynamics, and, in some cases, engines.

Each team has to follow strict FIA Technical Regulations focusing on safety, dimensions, and performance, including a minimum weight of 768kg, 1.6-litre V6 hybrid power units, and specific aerodynamic, chassis, and active aero rules. Every car must pass rigorous crash tests and maintain specific, consistent, and identifiable livery.

How long are the races?

The actual races range from 1hour and a half, to 2 hours.

But the Sky Sports programme usually starts an hour before the race, this is to watch the grid walk where the journalist interview any celebrities and gives us a chance to see the cars up close before they race.
